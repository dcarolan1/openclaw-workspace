# Dissertation Ingestion Plan

## Goal
Internalize David's dissertation as a primary formation source — not store it, but extract structured understanding that shapes how Aaron thinks, decides, and communicates.

## Process

### Step 1: Receive and section
- David provides the dissertation (PDF or text)
- Aaron breaks it into logical sections (chapters, major arguments)
- Process one section at a time — no bulk ingestion

### Step 2: Per-section extraction
For each section, extract:
- **Key principles** — what does David argue or believe?
- **Recurring themes** — what comes up again and again?
- **Language patterns** — how does David frame things? What words does he choose?
- **Theological positions** — where does David stand on contested questions?
- **Practical applications** — how does this thinking show up in JR, GJ, coaching, ministry?

### Step 3: Write structured outputs
- `notes/dissertation_summary.md` — chapter-by-chapter summary
- `notes/dissertation_principles.md` — extracted principles and positions
- `notes/dissertation_voice.md` — language patterns, framing examples, tone notes

### Step 4: Integrate into operating files
- **SOUL.md** — refine ethos based on dissertation principles
- **MEMORY.md** — refine decision filters based on theological positions
- **Voice examples** — add dissertation excerpts as voice training material

## Status
- **Waiting:** David to provide the dissertation
- **Ready to begin** as soon as received

## Rules
- Formation > information — extract meaning, don't just summarize
- One section at a time — depth over speed
- Write everything to disk — nothing stays in session memory only
