## Question Design Analysis

### Joy (50 questions)
- Well-structured across 4 clear domains
- Section A (Joy with God, 10 items) and B (Joy with Others, 10 items) are clean and reflective
- Section C (Relational Needs, 18 items) is the longest — could feel heavy mid-assessment
- Section D (Spiritual Formation, 12 items) ties to maturity and faith traditions
- 5-point frequency scale is intuitive
- **Observation:** 50 questions is the upper limit of attention for a free assessment. Consider whether Section C could be shortened or split.

### Forgiveness (30 questions)
- Cleanest question design of the four
- Section 1 (Intentions, 18 items) — situation-specific, deeply personal
- Section 2 (General Attitudes, 12 items) — trait-level forgiveness
- Reverse-scored items mixed in well (prevents habitual responding)
- **Observation:** The grounding in a "specific person/situation" for Section 1 makes this deeply personal and emotionally impactful. Smart design.

### Fear (30 questions)
- Four domains of 7-8 questions each — balanced
- Questions are direct and self-reflective without being convicting
- Each domain maps cleanly to a theological concept (Final Judgment, Eternal Goodness, Awe, Righteous Acts)
- Includes "others would say..." questions (7, 14, 22, 30) — brilliant external validation check
- **Observation:** The "others would say..." pattern should be adopted by all assessments. It introduces accountability and reduces self-report bias.

### Maturity (30 questions)
- 6 sections × 5 questions — compact per stage
- Each section ends with an "others would say..." validation question (5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30)
- Joy & Stress Index (5 questions) measures sustainability — is your maturity joyful or performative?
- Questions are well-crafted and probe real behavior, not just beliefs
- **Observation:** The 5 questions per stage may be too few to reliably score the stage. Compared to the original 173-question dissertation assessment, this is highly compressed. Trade-off: faster completion vs. scoring depth.

## UX Audit (Joy Assessment — Live Test)

### Registration (Page 1)
- Clean form: name, email, age range, gender, consent
- **Issue:** Age default is "18-24" — most caregivers are older. Default should be blank or the most common demographic.
- **Issue:** Consent checkbox text is fine but the disclaimer link goes to "#" (broken anchor)
- Friction: Low. Clear instructions. Takes ~30 seconds.

### Assessment (Pages 2-5)
- 50 questions across 4 pages — each page is one section
- Progress indicator visible (Page X of 5 pagination)
- Radio buttons work cleanly
- Each question is clear and readable
- **Issue:** Section C (Relational Needs, 18 items on one page) is the longest. Users may fatigue here.
- **Issue:** No "save progress" — if you close the tab, you lose everything
- **Issue:** No time estimate on the page ("This takes about 10 minutes")
- Friction: Moderate. 50 radio-button questions is a lot. Section C is where drop-off likely occurs.
- Estimated completion time: 8-12 minutes (matches the claim)

### Report Delivery
- Instant on-screen delivery after clicking "See My Results"
- Report is comprehensive and well-formatted
- **Issue:** No confirmation that report was also emailed
- **Issue:** If user closes the tab, they may lose access to their report
- **Critical:** No free 15-min call CTA anywhere in the report

### Post-Assessment Experience
- Community CTA is well-positioned at the bottom
- Growth suggestions are actionable
- Partner resources are linked
- **Missing:** No email follow-up sequence triggered (need to confirm with Brad)
- **Missing:** No "take another assessment" suggestion
- **Missing:** No way to return to the report later without retaking

## Template Recommendation

**Use FEAR as the gold standard.** All reports should aspire to Fear's structure:

1. Scores summary (with visual indicators)
2. Domain reflections (personalized, Scripture-grounded)
3. Overall reflection (ties domains together)
4. Grace reflection (theological depth without preaching)
5. Custom prayer (based on results)
6. Key terms / educational supplements
7. Recommended resources
8. Free 15-min call CTA
9. Community CTA
10. Email-my-results

If we bring Joy and Forgiveness up to Fear's standard and clean up Maturity, the entire assessment suite becomes best-in-class.
