# Dissertation Principles — David B. Carolan

## Nine Principles of Christian Joy
1. God is holy and has presented covenants He fulfills — He speaks and we listen
2. God made us with relational needs (not weaknesses) to be met by Him and through others
3. Love and joy are in spontaneous, primary relationship in the Godhead
4. Acts of love bundled with joy produce authenticity; duty apart from love lacks joy
5. Satan steals joy through deception, accusation, and substitution
6. As God "re-images" Himself in us, we experience the attraction of love and joy
7. Joy is a love emotion that spurs intimate human interaction → maturity
8. The body responds to joy profoundly (right prefrontal cortex)
9. We are wired for joy-bonding with God and others

## Core Theological Positions
- **Sanctification:** Modified Reformed-Keswick. Definitive sanctification preferred over progressive. Combined with charismatic openness and Wesleyan emphasis on experiential encounters.
- **Joy:** Not circumstantial but relational. A commanded emotion, not optional. The best measure of maturity.
- **Fear:** God uses fear to gain attention and lead to love (the "hesed methodology"), but human fear is always destructive — based on mistrust and manipulation.
- **Maturity (R.A.R.E.):** Remain relational, Act like yourself, Return to joy, Endure hardship well. Speed of returning to joy from negative emotions is the single biggest indicator of maturity.
- **Obedience:** Without joy is unacceptable to God (Deut 28:47-48). God requires joyful, cheerful, grateful compliance — not dutiful obligation.
- **Aloneness:** More fundamental than loneliness — a state of disconnection from love. The core problem joy solves.
- **Contemplative tradition:** The preferred formation pathway for experiencing God's presence.

## Key Distinctions David Makes
- **Joy vs. happiness:** Joy is relational and enduring; happiness is circumstantial and temporary
- **Impetus vs. intention:** Joy produces the *power* behind action, not just the *decision* to act
- **Joy-bonds vs. fear-bonds:** The binary framework for all relational dynamics
- **"Taught" vs. "caught":** Joy is experiential, not primarily intellectual
- **"Thinking with God" vs. "thinking about God":** Relationship vs. theology about relationship
- **"Remaining joyful" vs. episodic joy:** Steady-state maturity, not emotional spikes

## Practical Principles
- Joy-bonding becomes permanent within 30 days of consistent practice
- 19 brain-friendly joy skills based on biblical principles
- Four requirements for developing joy-bonds: (1) willingness to experience God humbly, (2) Bible-centeredness, (3) hearing God's still small voice, (4) consistent joyful interaction with others
- The speed of "return to joy" from negative emotions is the key maturity metric

## Assessment Expertise Context

- **Joy:** David's core expertise. Dissertation-level research. Recognized authority.
- **Forgiveness:** Grounded in Oxford summer studies with Dr. Liz Guilford and Dr. Everett Worthington. Academically solid.
