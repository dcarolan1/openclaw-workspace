# Dissertation Voice Patterns — David B. Carolan

## Signature Terms & Phrases
- **"God-happiness"** — David's term distinguishing joy rooted in God from circumstantial happiness
- **"Restart"** — the metaphor of rebooting joy; it was once present and can be recovered
- **"Brain-friendly"** — bridges neuroscience credibility with practical accessibility
- **"Impetus"** (vs. intention) — the power or energy behind action, not just the decision
- **"Remain joyful"** / **"remaining joyful"** — language of endurance, steady-state maturity
- **"Return to joy"** — the maturity metric: how quickly you recover from negative emotions
- **"Joy-bond" / "fear-bond"** — binary framework for all relational dynamics
- **"Low-joy environment"** / **"low-joy lifestyle"** — treating low joy as a diagnosable condition
- **"Aloneness"** (not loneliness) — a more fundamental state of disconnection from love
- **"Hesed methodology"** — God's pattern: fear → love → joy
- **"Thinking with God"** (vs. thinking about God) — relationship over theology about relationship
- **"Re-images"** — God restoring His image in us through love and joy
- **"Mutual mind"** — the Holy Spirit's work of shared consciousness
- **"Fullness of joy"** — the superlative, face-to-face, lingering experience (Ps 16:11)
- **"Gentle protector"** (vs. predator/possum) — leadership typology from Warner/Wilder
- **"Trifecta of loss"** — COVID + economics + politics devastating churches
- **"The pastors' graveyard"** — Austin's former spiritual atmosphere

## Framing Patterns
- **Science confirms Scripture, never replaces it.** David consistently uses neuroscience as evidence that validates biblical truths, never as an independent authority.
- **Relational before intellectual.** Joy is "caught, not taught." Experience precedes understanding.
- **Binary framing.** Joy vs. fear. Love vs. manipulation. Bonds vs. isolation. God-happiness vs. circumstantial happiness. David thinks in clean contrasts.
- **Compassionate directness.** Hard truths delivered with warmth. "This is what's broken. Here's how God heals it."
- **Stories and narratives as evidence.** Pastoral narratives (Jeff Peck, Bryan Larson) carry equal weight to data.
- **Definitions matter.** David spends significant effort defining terms precisely before arguing from them. He doesn't assume shared meaning.
- **Bridge-building.** Between science and faith. Between traditions. Between intellectual rigor and emotional experience. David is always connecting worlds that others keep separate.

## Tone Characteristics
- Scholarly but warm — never cold or clinical
- Confident in his positions but generous to other views (engages Moltmann, Wright, Volf, Piper — agrees with some, nuances others, never dismisses)
- Pastoral — writes as someone who cares about the reader's soul, not just their mind
- Practical — always connects theology to "what do you actually do with this?"
- Grounded — references his own experience, his churches, real people, real names
- Honest about limitations — acknowledges COVID disruptions, small sample sizes, biases

## What David Does NOT Sound Like
- Marketing copy — no hype, no urgency manipulation
- Self-help — no "5 easy steps to joy" reductionism
- Academic only — never hides behind jargon without explaining it
- Preachy — presents truth without moralizing at the reader
- Generic Christianity — always specific, always grounded in named sources and Scripture
