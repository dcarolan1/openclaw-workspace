# Dissertation Summary — David B. Carolan

## Identification
- **Title:** Enhancing Spiritual Formation by Increasing Joy Bonds in and between Congregants
- **Author:** David B. Carolan
- **Institution:** South University Online, College of Theology
- **Degree:** Doctor of Ministry
- **Year:** June 2022
- **Advisor:** Mark S. Davenport, D.Min.
- **Public-facing name:** "The Joy Restart Project" (joyrestart.com)

## Overall Thesis (2-3 sentences)
By increasing church congregants' capacity for joy through self-paced learning and practicing brain-friendly joy-building skills consistent with spiritual formation objectives, they will become more relational, authentic, and able to endure hardship. Joy is not merely a byproduct of the Christian life but a commanded, measurable, love-rooted emotion that, when increased, produces the *impetus* (not merely the *intention*) for spiritual transformation — and the best measure of Christian maturity is the ability to remain joyful in all circumstances. The contemplative tradition of spiritual formation, combined with neuroscientific insights about how the brain processes joy, offers the most effective pathway for restoring joy even during seasons of isolation and cultural upheaval.

## Chapter 1: Introduction
- Joy and spiritual transformation must go together, especially during hardship
- Happiness is an inferior substitute for joy — it disguises aloneness but doesn't heal it
- Fear steals joy and ruins the body; they cannot coexist
- Maturity is best measured by joyfulness, not Bible knowledge or activity level
- Joy was once present and can be recovered ("restart")

## Chapter 2: Community and Church Context
- COVID-19 + economic upheaval + political crisis = "trifecta of loss" devastating churches
- Social distancing caused measurable harm to attachment, brain function, emotional well-being
- Church leaders made fear-based decisions mirroring the world instead of offering a Christian response
- Aloneness (not just loneliness) is the core human problem joy solves
- Affluence and individualism create unique barriers to joy
- JR positioned as a bridge program for new/marginalized members toward deeper discipleship

## Chapter 3: Theology of Community and Ministry
- Joy is relational, not circumstantial — a primary emotion originating in loving attachment
- The brain has a specific joy center (right prefrontal cortex) responding to loving personality
- God's hesed (loyal, covenantal love) is the anchor — love always precedes and produces joy
- Remaining joyful is the best measure of maturity (R.A.R.E. framework)
- Nine Principles of Christian Joy defined
- Working definition: "Joy is a powerful, responsive spiritual emotion derived from a loving relationship to another person whereby peace and well-being (shalom) have been previously attained (or are hopefully expected) causing a virtuous state of mind that will demonstrate by thought, word, and deed: 'I delight in you.'"
