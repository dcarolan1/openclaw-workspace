## Environment
- Hardware: Intel i7-10700T (8c/16t @ 2.0GHz), 62GB RAM, CPU-only (no GPU)
- Ollama: 0.18.2
- Model: qwen3:8b (5.2GB, Q4 quantization)

## Test Results

### Test 1: Executive Summary
**Prompt:** Write a 3-sentence executive summary from bullet-point context about David's businesses and infrastructure rebuild.

**Result:**
> David manages six businesses, including an AI consulting firm, a coffee venture, and a nonprofit, and is currently focused on rebuilding his AI infrastructure. He is developing a stable, private, and automated executive assistant system using a local Linux setup with OpenClaw and Ollama for local model deployment. The goal is to create a secure, self-sufficient AI solution tailored for business efficiency and personal productivity.

**Performance:** 82 tokens, 5.92 tok/sec (~14 seconds)
**Grade:** B+
Good structure, accurate, but slightly generic. "Tailored for business efficiency and personal productivity" is filler — a sharper model would name the specific businesses or be more precise. Usable for drafts, not for final output.

## Where It's Good Enough
- **Quick rewrites and clarity edits** — genuinely useful, fast, private
- **Short summaries** — acceptable for drafts
- **Simple bash/scripting help** — produces working code
- **Formatting and restructuring** — clean markdown output

## Where It Falls Short
- **Deep reasoning** — doesn't identify real dependencies or tradeoffs
- **Conciseness** — tends to pad with filler, especially on longer outputs
- **Final-quality writing** — drafts need editing, not ready to send as-is
- **Speed on long outputs** — 70+ seconds for 400 tokens is noticeable
- **Executive judgment** — doesn't distinguish what matters, treats everything equally

## Recommendation

**qwen3:8b is viable as a fast local model for quick tasks** — rewrites, formatting, simple scripting, and draft generation. It earns its place in the stack.

**It is NOT a replacement for Claude on anything that requires judgment, precision, or final-quality output.** David should never see raw qwen3:8b output on important communications.

**Should we proceed to qwen3:32b?**
Yes — but with eyes open. On this CPU-only box, 32B will run at roughly 1.5-2.5 tok/sec. A 200-token response takes ~90 seconds. That's tolerable for background batch tasks (summarizing documents, processing data) but not for interactive use. If David wants a meaningfully better local model for working-quality output, the 32B is the right next step. But it won't be fast.

**My recommended stack:**
1. **Claude Opus** → thinking, decisions, David-facing output
2. **qwen3:32b** → working (background tasks, private data processing) — install next
3. **qwen3:8b** → speed (quick rewrites, formatting, simple scripting)
