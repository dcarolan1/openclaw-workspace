# Maturity Report — Upgraded Language

**Template standard:** FEAR report (clear domain scores, grace-filled narratives, Scripture, custom prayer)
**Goal:** Simplify the 8-stage model presentation while preserving its depth. Make it readable for someone who has never heard of the Life Model.

---

## Structural Improvements

### Current problems:
1. Too many concepts introduced at once (stages, cutting edge, active vs future, JSI)
2. Language is dense and sometimes circular
3. "Cutting edge (C2.0)" terminology is undefined for the user
4. Active vs Future stage concept is powerful but poorly explained
5. Nazarene 8-phase table is rich but overwhelming in a report context

### Proposed structure (match Fear report flow):
1. **Scoring summary** — one clear table showing all stages + JSI
2. **Your current stage** — identify the primary stage with a warm, clear narrative
3. **Stage-by-stage reflections** — only the stages that are relevant (not all 8)
4. **Joy & Stress Index** — is your maturity sustainable or performative?
5. **Growth suggestions** — specific, practical
6. **Custom prayer**
7. **Free call CTA**
8. **Next assessment CTA**
9. **Community CTA**

## Upgraded Stage Narratives

### How to Read Your Stages

Everyone develops through stages of maturity — from receiving love (Infant) through spiritual wisdom (Elder). These aren't labels or judgments. They describe the relational skills you've developed and where God is inviting your next step.

Most adults in our culture are still developing skills from earlier stages — and that's not failure. It's the reality of growing up in a world that doesn't always teach us how to be relational, authentic, and joyful under stress. Recognizing where you are is the beginning of growth, not the end.

The five core stages are: **Infant** (receiving love), **Child** (relational stability), **Adult** (personal responsibility), **Parent** (empathy and guidance), and **Elder** (spiritual wisdom). Our culture has fractured these stages in ways that create nuanced expressions — people who are chronologically adult but relationally stuck in earlier patterns. Your report shows where you're strong and where growth is waiting.

---

### Infant — Receiving Love

**Fully Mastered:**
You rest securely in being loved — by God and by others. You welcome comfort, receive care with gratitude, and feel safe even at rest. This foundation is solid and joyful. "See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God" (1 John 3:1).

**Refinement:**
You receive love and comfort reliably in most situations. This foundation is strong. God is gently refining your ability to rest without guilt and to welcome care even during stress.

**Cultivating:**
You're growing in your ability to receive love. You believe you're loved, but comfort is sometimes resisted or minimized. Practice welcoming care without guilt — this is the foundation everything else builds on.

**Inviting Growth:**
You often withdraw or self-soothe alone when hurt. God invites you to let Him and safe people into your pain. Receiving love is not weakness — it's the starting point for all maturity. "Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest" (Matthew 11:28).

---

### Child — Relational Stability

**Fully Mastered:**
You stay connected when corrected or disappointed. You express needs calmly and respect limits while still feeling loved. Your relational base is firm and joyful.

**Refinement:**
You handle correction and disappointment well most of the time. God is refining your ability to stay fully connected in high-stress moments and to receive correction with gratitude.

**Cultivating:**
You're developing the ability to stay connected when things don't go your way. Frustration or withdrawal may still surface when corrected or disappointed. Keep practicing quick repair — steady growth is happening.

**Inviting Growth:**
Correction, disappointment, or limits often trigger resentment or withdrawal. God invites you to practice staying connected and expressing needs respectfully. This is a grace-filled place to grow relational stability. "A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger" (Proverbs 15:1).

---

### Adult — Personal Responsibility

**Fully Mastered:**
You take ownership quickly when you've hurt someone. You balance caring for others with caring for yourself, and you choose connection over being right. This is healthy, mature responsibility.

**Refinement:**
You take ownership and restore relationships promptly in most situations. God is refining your ability to balance self-care and other-care during seasons of overload.

**Cultivating:**
You're growing in taking ownership and restoring relationships. Repair may sometimes be delayed, or self-care and other-care may feel out of balance. God invites humility and prompt confession — growth is happening.

**Inviting Growth:**
Taking ownership or admitting fault may feel threatening. You may avoid repair, blame others, or struggle when criticized. God invites you to practice simple ownership — "I was wrong, I'm sorry" — and to seek accountability. "If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just and will forgive us" (1 John 1:9).

---

### Parent — Empathy & Guidance

**Fully Mastered:**
You guide others gently without resentment. Your care flows from joy, not duty. Those you mentor feel safe and empowered to grow. This is beautiful, sustainable overflow.

**Refinement:**
You guide and correct others with warmth in most situations. God is refining your ability to balance rest and service so your care remains joyful and sustainable.

**Cultivating:**
You're beginning to guide others with warmth and patience. Fatigue, over-control, or serving from duty rather than joy may surface at times. This is a hopeful emerging stage — prioritize rest.

**Inviting Growth:**
You may serve from obligation or resentment, or over-control when guiding others. God invites you to prioritize rest and practice care that flows from joy rather than exhaustion. "Come to me, all you who are weary" applies here too.

---

### Elder — Spiritual Wisdom

**Fully Mastered:**
You bring calm and hope in anxious situations. You enjoy mentoring and releasing others into leadership. You speak blessing, value shared responsibility, and others describe your presence as peaceful, steady, and wise.

**Refinement:**
You bring calm, blessing, and shared authority in many difficult moments. God is refining your ability to fully release recognition and multiply shared leadership.

**Cultivating:**
You're developing the ability to bring calm and blessing in anxious situations. Releasing control or seeking recognition may still surface. Keep practicing shared authority — Elder wisdom is emerging.

**Inviting Growth:**
You may seek recognition, hold control, or hesitate to release others into leadership. God invites you to practice blessing and shared authority. Elder wisdom begins with letting go. "The greatest among you will be your servant" (Matthew 23:11).

---

### Joy & Stress Index (JSI)

**Fully Mastered:**
Joy flows naturally and sustainably. You feel peace and strength even in hard situations. Mature kindness feels effortless — a sign that your maturity is genuine, not performed.

**Refinement:**
Joy is strong in most situations, but occasional pressure or depletion invites small adjustments. Practices of rest and gratitude will sustain your maturity more effortlessly.

**Cultivating:**
Joy is growing. You recover well after caring for others much of the time, and mature kindness increasingly feels natural. Balancing rest and service will help sustain your growth.

**Inviting Growth:**
You may often feel depleted, pressured to perform, or find it hard to return to peace after hard situations. This is common when earlier foundations are still developing. God invites you to begin receiving comfort and building joy through gratitude and safe relationships.

Grow [First Name] in maturity — not by striving, but by remaining joyful in Your presence.

In Jesus' name,
Amen.
