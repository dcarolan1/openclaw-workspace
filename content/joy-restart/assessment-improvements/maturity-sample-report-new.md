## SUPPLEMENT: UNDERSTANDING THE MATURITY STAGES

### The Five Core Stages

Everyone develops through five stages of maturity. Each one builds on the one before it — like floors of a building. If an earlier floor has gaps, the floors above it require more effort to maintain.

| Stage | Core Capacity | What It Looks Like |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Receiving Love (Infant) | Ability to receive care, comfort, and love without guilt | Resting in being loved. Welcoming help. Feeling safe. |
| Relational Stability (Child) | Ability to stay connected when corrected or disappointed | Handling "no" without shutting down. Expressing needs calmly. |
| Personal Responsibility (Adult) | Ability to own mistakes and balance self/other care | Quick repair. Choosing connection over being right. |
| Empathy & Guidance (Parent) | Ability to care for others from joy, not duty | Guiding without controlling. Resting so care flows from overflow. |
| Spiritual Wisdom (Elder) | Ability to bring calm, speak blessing, release control | Mentoring others into leadership. Valuing shared authority over recognition. |

### Why 8 Stages?

The five stages above describe the foundational capacities God designed us to develop. In our culture, however, these stages often get fractured — people develop some skills within a stage while missing others, creating what we call "trapped stages."

Our expanded model identifies nuances within each stage that help explain why a chronological adult might still react like a child under stress — not because of weakness, but because their culture didn't teach them the relational skills that stage requires.

**Healthy expression of a trapped stage:** Learning these skills later in life, with patience and safe community.
**Unhealthy expression:** Chronic patterns of withdrawal, reactivity, control, or performance that the person can't explain.

Your coach or counselor can use this deeper view to guide your growth more precisely.
