## YOUR JOURNEY — Where You Are and Why It Matters

Maturity doesn't follow a calendar. A person can be 50 years old and still be developing the relational skills that should have been learned at 5 — not because of failure, but because no one taught them. Our culture rewards independence, performance, and toughness. It rarely teaches us how to receive love, stay connected under stress, or guide others from joy instead of duty.

That's why this report may feel surprising — or even hard to read. If your strongest stage is somewhere you didn't expect, or your growth area feels uncomfortably basic, that's not a verdict. It's clarity. And clarity is where God does His best work.

Here's what we want you to understand about your results:

**Personal Responsibility — Refinement** 🟢🟢🟢🟢⚪

You take ownership quickly when you've hurt someone. You balance caring for others with caring for yourself, and you stay kind even under criticism in most situations. This is a real strength — and it shows maturity that many people never develop.

God is refining this in you — not building from scratch, but polishing what's already strong. The invitation here is to maintain this even during seasons of overload, when the temptation is to protect yourself at the cost of connection.

People who know you well probably see someone who is reliable, honest, and willing to admit when they're wrong. That's not common. It's a gift.

### Where Growth Is Waiting

**Relational Stability — Inviting Growth** 🟢⚪⚪⚪⚪

Correction, disappointment, or limits often trigger frustration or withdrawal in your relationships right now. You may find yourself shutting down, reacting strongly, or pulling away when things don't go as expected. This isn't a character flaw — it's a sign that relational stability is still being built.

Many adults in our culture are still developing these skills. We grow up in environments that don't always teach us how to stay connected when things go wrong. Recognizing this is not failure — it's the beginning of real growth.

**Here's what's important:** this is likely the single most impactful area for your overall maturity. When relational stability strengthens, everything above it — your responsibility, your empathy, even your spiritual wisdom — becomes more sustainable and less effortful.

God invites you to practice staying connected — even when it's uncomfortable. Start small: the next time you feel the urge to withdraw after being corrected, pause and stay present. One honest conversation with a safe person can begin to rewire this pattern.

"A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger" (Proverbs 15:1).

**Spiritual Wisdom — Inviting Growth** 🟢🟢⚪⚪⚪

You may seek recognition, hold control, or hesitate to release others into leadership. This is normal — Spiritual Wisdom is the last stage to develop, and most people are still growing into it regardless of age.

**This is a future stage for you right now** — meaning it will mature naturally as the stages below it strengthen. You don't need to force Elder-level wisdom. You need to build the relational stability and empathy that make wisdom sustainable.

Think of it like a building. You can put furniture on the fifth floor before the second floor is finished — but everything feels unstable. When the lower floors are strong, the upper floors hold weight effortlessly.

**There is no rush.** God is not on a deadline with your maturity. He is faithfully carrying you regardless of what stage you're in. The goal isn't to reach Spiritual Wisdom as fast as possible. The goal is to be fully present — joyful, relational, authentic — wherever you are right now.

"The greatest among you will be your servant" (Matthew 23:11).

## GROWTH SUGGESTIONS

**Daily:**
When you feel the urge to withdraw or shut down, pause for 10 seconds before responding. Name what you're feeling — even silently. This interrupts the pattern and creates space for a different choice.

**Weekly:**
Have one honest conversation with a safe person about something that bothered you. Practice staying connected through it — not fixing it, not defending yourself, just staying present.

**Relational:**
Ask someone who knows you well: "Do I tend to pull away when things get hard?" Listen to their answer without defending. Their observation is data, not accusation.

**Spiritual:**
Spend 5 minutes daily in stillness, asking God: "Where are You inviting me to stay connected instead of withdrawing?" Listen for the still small voice — not for an answer, but for His presence.

## SUPPLEMENT: UNDERSTANDING THE MATURITY STAGES

*This section provides deeper context for those who want to understand the framework behind the assessment. It's also useful for coaches, counselors, and ministry leaders who use this tool with the people they serve.*

---

### The Five Core Stages

Everyone develops through five stages of maturity. Each one builds on the one before it — like floors of a building. If an earlier floor has gaps, the floors above it require more effort to maintain.

| Stage | Core Capacity | What It Looks Like When Healthy |
|-------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Receiving Love** (Infant) | Ability to receive care, comfort, and love without guilt | Resting in being loved. Welcoming help. Feeling safe even at rest. |
| **Relational Stability** (Child) | Ability to stay connected when corrected or disappointed | Handling "no" without shutting down. Expressing needs calmly. Staying present in conflict. |
| **Personal Responsibility** (Adult) | Ability to own mistakes and balance self/other care | Quick repair after hurt. Choosing connection over being right. Staying kind under criticism. |
| **Empathy & Guidance** (Parent) | Ability to care for others from joy, not duty | Guiding without controlling. Correcting with warmth. Resting so care flows from overflow. |
| **Spiritual Wisdom** (Elder) | Ability to bring calm, speak blessing, release control | Mentoring others into leadership. Valuing shared authority. Bringing peace to anxious situations. |

---

### Why 8 Stages? — How Our Culture Fractures Maturity

The five stages above describe the foundational capacities God designed us to develop. In most traditional cultures throughout history — First Nations, African, Asian, Middle Eastern — these stages were supported by community rituals, mentoring relationships, and clear expectations at each phase of life.

In our modern Western culture, however, these stages often get fractured. Children are pressured into adult responsibilities before they've learned to receive love. Adults are rewarded for independence before they've learned relational stability. Leaders are promoted before they've developed empathy.

The result: people develop some skills within a stage while missing others. A 45-year-old can be brilliant at their job (Adult-level responsibility) while still reacting to criticism like a 7-year-old (Child-level relational stability). This isn't a character defect. It's a cultural reality.

Our expanded 8-stage model identifies three additional nuances within the traditional five stages:

| Additional Stage | What It Captures |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Autonomy within Infant** | The difference between healthy dependence (receiving love freely) and shame-driven independence (refusing to need anyone) |
| **Initiative within Child** | The difference between healthy exploration (trying new things with security) and guilt-driven passivity (afraid to act without permission) |
| **Identity within Adolescent** | The difference between secure identity (knowing who you are in Christ) and role confusion (performing different selves for different audiences) |

These nuances help explain why a person might score well in some areas of a stage but struggle in others. Your coach or counselor can use this deeper view to guide your growth more precisely.

---

### Healthy vs. Unhealthy Expressions of Trapped Stages

When a stage is "trapped" — meaning it wasn't fully developed at the expected time — it shows up in predictable patterns:

**Trapped at Receiving Love:**
- *Healthy expression:* Learning to receive love later in life, building trust slowly but intentionally with safe people
- *Unhealthy expression:* Chronic self-sufficiency, refusing comfort, guilt about needing others, inability to rest

**Trapped at Relational Stability:**
- *Healthy expression:* Recognizing the withdrawal pattern and practicing repair with a trusted friend or counselor
- *Unhealthy expression:* Shutting down after correction, silent treatment, explosive reactions to small disappointments, chronic conflict avoidance

**Trapped at Personal Responsibility:**
- *Healthy expression:* Learning to own mistakes later in life, developing the humility to say "I was wrong"
- *Unhealthy expression:* Blame-shifting, defensiveness, inability to balance self-care with caring for others, needing to be right

**Trapped at Empathy & Guidance:**
- *Healthy expression:* Developing joyful care through rest and boundaries, learning to guide without controlling
- *Unhealthy expression:* Burnout, resentment, over-functioning, martyr complex, "I do everything and no one appreciates it"

**Trapped at Spiritual Wisdom:**
- *Healthy expression:* Gradually releasing control and mentoring others into leadership
- *Unhealthy expression:* Holding power, seeking recognition, unable to bless others' success, critical spirit

### The Joy & Stress Index — What It Tells You

The Joy & Stress Index measures the sustainability of your maturity. It answers one critical question: **Is your mature behavior flowing from joy, or is it costing you energy?**

- **When joy is high and stress is low:** Maturity feels natural. Kindness, patience, and responsibility aren't effort — they're overflow. This is what "remaining joyful" looks like.
- **When joy is low and stress is high:** Maturity feels performative. You're doing the right things, but they drain you. Over time, this leads to burnout, resentment, or sudden regressions under pressure.

The single most important factor in improving your JSI: **strengthen the lowest stage in your report.** When foundations are solid, everything above them requires less effort. Joy replaces striving.
