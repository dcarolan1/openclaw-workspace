## Upgraded Domain Narratives

### Overall Joy Index

**High (70-100%):**
Your scores reflect a life where joy is present and active — in your relationship with God, in your connections with others, and in how you engage the world around you. This doesn't mean life is easy. It means you've developed the capacity to remain joyful even when it's hard. As Scripture reminds us, "The joy of the Lord is your strength" (Nehemiah 8:10). Continue building on this foundation — joy deepens as it's shared.

**Moderate (40-69%):**
Your scores suggest that joy is present in your life but isn't yet consistent across every area. Some domains are stronger than others — and that's normal. Joy is not a fixed trait; it's a capacity that grows through relationship and practice. God is already at work in the areas where your joy is strongest. The areas where it's lower are not failures — they're invitations. "He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion" (Philippians 1:6).

**Low (0-39%):**
Your scores suggest that joy may feel distant right now — and that takes honesty to acknowledge. Whether the cause is circumstances, relational pain, spiritual dryness, or something deeper, know this: joy can be restored. It was never meant to depend on your strength alone. "The Lord your God is with you, the Mighty Warrior who saves. He will take great delight in you; in His love He will no longer rebuke you, but will rejoice over you with singing" (Zephaniah 3:17). You are not behind. You are exactly where God can meet you.

### Joy with God

**High (70-100%):**
Your relationship with God is a primary source of joy in your life. Scripture, prayer, worship, and sensing His guidance regularly fill you with delight. This is the foundation everything else builds on — when joy with God is strong, joy with others and resilience under stress grow naturally. "In Your presence there is fullness of joy; at Your right hand are pleasures forevermore" (Psalm 16:11).

**Moderate (40-69%):**
You experience joy with God, but it may come and go depending on circumstances or seasons. Some spiritual practices bring you joy more than others, and there may be moments when God's presence feels distant. This is a normal part of the journey — not a sign of failure. God invites you to lean into the practices that connect you most deeply to Him. "Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you" (James 4:8).

**Low (0-39%):**
Joy with God may feel distant or inconsistent right now. That can be painful — especially if you remember a time when it was different. Whatever has created that distance — doubt, hurt, busyness, or something you can't name — God has not moved. He is waiting, and He delights in you even when you can't feel it. "The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit" (Psalm 34:18). Start small: one honest prayer, one moment of stillness. Joy with God is caught, not earned.

### Joy with Others

**High (70-100%):**
Your relationships are a genuine source of joy. You share joyful moments with others, help others find joy, and can hold both joy and sorrow in your connections. This is a sign of relational maturity — the ability to remain relational, authentic, and present even in difficulty. "Rejoice with those who rejoice; mourn with those who mourn" (Romans 12:15). Your joy blesses the people around you.

**Moderate (40-69%):**
You experience joy in some relationships but not consistently across all of them. You may find it easier to give joy than to receive it, or certain relationships may drain more than they fill. This is common — and it's where growth in relational skills can make the biggest difference. Joy with others is built through intentional, safe connection. "As iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another" (Proverbs 27:17).

**Low (0-39%):**
Joy in relationships may feel scarce right now. You may feel isolated, misunderstood, or drained by the people around you. This isn't a character flaw — it's often a sign that important relational needs are going unmet. God designed you for connection, and when that connection is missing, joy suffers. "It is not good for the man to be alone" (Genesis 2:18). The first step is finding one safe person — or one safe community — where you can begin to be seen and known.

---

### Relational Needs

**High (70-100%):**
Your relational needs are being met at a healthy level. You feel accepted, encouraged, secure, and cared for in your closest relationships. This creates a strong foundation for joy — when our God-given needs are met, joy flows naturally. Continue investing in the relationships that meet these needs, and be intentional about meeting the needs of others.

**Moderate (40-69%):**
Some of your relational needs are being met, but others are not. Your report identifies the two needs that are least fulfilled right now. These gaps directly affect your overall joy — especially joy with others. Addressing even one unmet need can create a ripple effect. Start with one trustworthy person and one honest conversation. "Carry each other's burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ" (Galatians 6:2).

**Low (0-39%):**
Several of your relational needs are significantly unmet. This is likely contributing to lower joy overall and may be creating feelings of loneliness or disconnection. This is not weakness — these are God-given needs that He designed to be met through relationship. Addressing them (even slowly) can dramatically increase your capacity for joy. Consider joining a community where encouragement and safety are prioritized. You were not meant to carry this alone.

---

### Spiritual Formation

**High (70-100%):**
Your spiritual practices are actively strengthening your joy. Gratitude, identity in Christ, worship across settings, and sharing your faith all contribute to a rich inner life. Continue to deepen these practices — and consider exploring contemplative traditions that may open new dimensions of experiencing God's presence.

**Moderate (40-69%):**
Your spiritual formation is growing but may lack consistency or depth in some areas. Perhaps gratitude comes naturally but contemplative prayer feels foreign, or you share your faith eagerly but struggle with stillness. God meets us through many pathways — the ones that feel less natural may be where the deepest growth is waiting. "Be still, and know that I am God" (Psalm 46:10).

**Low (0-39%):**
Spiritual practices may feel dry, obligatory, or disconnected from your actual life right now. This is more common than most people admit — and it doesn't mean your faith is failing. It often means your approach to formation needs to shift. Joy in spiritual life is caught through experience, not earned through discipline alone. Consider starting with one practice that feels accessible — a simple prayer of gratitude, a walk in creation, or a conversation with someone who radiates joy. God is already present in those moments.

Gracious Father,

Thank You for the joy You have already placed in [First Name]'s life — especially in [highest scoring domain].

Where joy with [lowest scoring domain area] feels distant or blocked right now, we ask for Your gentle hand. Not to fix what is broken, but to meet [First Name] exactly where they are.

[If lowest is Joy with God:]
Draw near, Lord. Remind them that Your presence is fullness of joy — not because they earned it, but because You delight in them.

[If lowest is Joy with Others:]
Bring safe people into their life. Give them the courage to be seen and known, and restore the joy that grows in honest relationship.

[If lowest is Relational Needs:]
Meet the needs that are going unmet. Open doors to community where encouragement, security, and care are real — not performed.

[If lowest is Spiritual Formation:]
Renew the practices that once brought life. Or reveal new ones. Let spiritual growth feel less like obligation and more like coming home.

We trust that You who began this good work will carry it to completion.

In Jesus' name,
Amen.
