## YOUR JOURNEY — Where You Are and Why It Matters

Maturity doesn't follow a calendar. A person can be 50 years old and still be developing the relational skills that should have been learned at 5 — not because of failure, but because no one taught them. Our culture rewards independence, performance, and toughness. It rarely teaches us how to receive love, stay connected under stress, or guide others from joy instead of duty.

If your report shows growth areas in stages that feel uncomfortably early — Infant or Child — you're not alone. Most adults carry gaps in these stages without knowing it. They compensate with intelligence, achievement, or spiritual activity. But underneath, the patterns are there.

That's not a verdict. It's clarity. And clarity is where God does His best work.

### Where Growth Is Waiting

**Child — Relational Stability: Inviting Growth** [X][ ][ ][ ][ ]

This is the ability to stay connected when corrected, disappointed, or told "no" — without shutting down, withdrawing, or reacting.

In the Life Model, the "Child" stage covers a wide range of development. To help you understand where within this stage you might be, it's useful to think of it in three parts:

**Early relational skills** (younger child) — the basics of boundaries, play, exploration, and the freedom to try new things safely. If these were limited, you may feel rigid or anxious about new relational experiences.

**Relational stability** (older child) — staying connected through conflict, expressing needs calmly, accepting limits while still feeling loved. If these were limited, you may withdraw or react strongly when things go wrong.

**Identity and values** (teen/adolescent) — knowing who you are apart from your roles, performance, and others' expectations. If this was limited, you may people-please, adapt to every environment, or struggle to articulate what you actually believe.

Any or all of these may be where your growth is waiting. Many people develop some of these skills while missing others — it depends on what your environment supported and what it didn't.

**Here's the key insight:** This is likely the single most impactful area for your overall maturity. When the Child stage strengthens — when you can stay connected under stress, know who you are, and express your needs without fear — everything above it becomes more sustainable and less effortful. Your Adult responsibility becomes less draining. Your parenting becomes more joyful. Even your future Elder wisdom gets a stronger foundation.

The path forward is not to skip ahead. God isn't asking you to go backward. He's asking you to let Him fill in what was missed — gently, in safe relationship, without shame. This is the most courageous work a person can do.

Start small: the next time you feel the urge to withdraw after being corrected, pause and stay present. One honest conversation with a safe person can begin to rewire this pattern.

"A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger" (Proverbs 15:1).

## GROWTH SUGGESTIONS

**Daily:**
When you feel the urge to withdraw or shut down, pause for 10 seconds before responding. Name what you're feeling — even silently. This interrupts the pattern and creates space for a different choice.

**Weekly:**
Have one honest conversation with a safe person about something that bothered you. Practice staying connected through it — not fixing it, not defending yourself, just staying present.

**Relational:**
Ask someone who knows you well: "Do I tend to pull away when things get hard?" Listen to their answer without defending. Their observation is data, not accusation.

**Spiritual:**
Spend 5 minutes daily in stillness, asking God: "Where are You inviting me to stay connected instead of withdrawing?" Listen for the still small voice — not for an answer, but for His presence.

**As a parent:**
Notice moments when you're parenting from depletion rather than joy. When you catch it, don't add guilt — add rest. Even 10 minutes of receiving care (from your spouse, a friend, or God directly) can shift the next hour of parenting from duty to delight.

## SUPPLEMENT: UNDERSTANDING THE MATURITY STAGES

*This section provides deeper context for those who want to understand the framework behind the assessment. It's also useful for coaches, counselors, and ministry leaders who use this tool with the people they serve.*

### The 5 Core Stages (Life Model Framework)

Dr. Jim Wilder's Life Model identifies 5 stages of maturity that describe the relational capacities God designed humans to develop:

| Stage | Core Capacity | What It Looks Like When Healthy |
|-------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Infant** | Receiving love, comfort, care without guilt | Resting in being loved. Welcoming help. Feeling safe even at rest. |
| **Child** | Staying connected under stress; knowing who you are | Handling correction without shutting down. Expressing needs calmly. Secure identity. |
| **Adult** | Owning mistakes; balancing self and other care | Quick repair after hurt. Choosing connection over being right. Staying kind under criticism. |
| **Parent** | Caring for others from joy, not duty | Guiding without controlling. Correcting with warmth. Resting so care flows from overflow. |
| **Elder** | Bringing calm, speaking blessing, releasing control | Mentoring others into leadership. Valuing shared authority. Bringing peace to anxious situations. |

---

### Why Joy Restart Uses 8 Stages

The 5 stages above are foundational — and they're what our assessment scores. But in our work with people, we've found that two of these stages (Child and Parent) contain such different developmental tasks that people often develop one part while missing another. We've also found that the Teen/Adolescent transition — which Wilder includes within the Child stage — deserves its own attention because our culture makes it especially difficult.

Here's how our 8 map to Wilder's 5:

| Wilder's 5 | Joy Restart's 8 | Why We Split |
|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Infant** | 1. **Receiving Love** | Same — the foundation of all maturity |
| **Child** | 2. **Early Relational Skills** (younger child) | Boundaries, play, exploration, safe experimentation |
| | 3. **Relational Stability** (older child) | Staying connected through conflict, expressing needs, accepting limits |
| | 4. **Identity & Values** (teen/adolescent) | Knowing who you are apart from roles and performance. Our culture traps many adults here. |
| **Adult** | 5. **Personal Responsibility** | Same — ownership, balance, choosing connection over being right |
| **Parent** | 6. **Hands-on Care** (younger parent) | Direct care of dependents. Parenting young children. Protecting, feeding, guiding. |
| | 7. **Empowering & Releasing** (older parent) | Releasing adult children, mentoring peers, shifting from doing to empowering |
| **Elder** | 8. **Spiritual Wisdom** | Same — blessing, shared authority, calm presence, mentoring leaders |

**The Joy & Stress Index** is not a stage — it's a multiplier that measures whether your maturity at any level is flowing from joy or costing you energy.

**Why this matters for you:** When your report shows the Child stage as a growth area, these three sub-stages help you understand *why*. You might think: "I'm not a child!" But when you see that it's specifically relational stability (staying connected under stress) or identity (knowing who you are apart from your roles), it often clicks: *"Oh — that's exactly what I struggle with."*

### What "Future Stages" Means

Your report may show one or more stages as "Future." This means:

- It's not where your growth energy should focus right now

**Example:** A 22-year-old single woman doesn't need to be focused on Elder wisdom. That's a future stage. Her growth energy is best invested in Relational Stability, Identity, and eventually Adult responsibility. Elder will come — and it will come more naturally because the foundation was built well.

Future stages aren't failures. They're horizons. God has not forgotten them.

### Healthy vs. Unhealthy Expressions of Each Stage

When a stage wasn't fully developed at the expected time, it shows up in predictable patterns:

**Infant — Receiving Love:**
- *Healthy growth:* Learning to receive love later in life, building trust slowly with safe people
- *Trapped pattern:* Chronic self-sufficiency, refusing comfort, guilt about needing others, inability to rest

**Child (younger) — Early Relational Skills:**
- *Healthy growth:* Rediscovering play, creativity, and exploration in safe adult relationships
- *Trapped pattern:* Rigidity, fear of new experiences, over-control of environment

**Child (older) — Relational Stability:**
- *Healthy growth:* Recognizing the withdrawal pattern and practicing staying connected
- *Trapped pattern:* Shutting down after correction, silent treatment, explosive reactions, chronic conflict avoidance

**Teen — Identity & Values:**
- *Healthy growth:* Exploring faith, values, and identity in safe community without fear
- *Trapped pattern:* People-pleasing, chameleon behavior, not knowing what you actually believe, identity built on roles or performance

**Adult — Personal Responsibility:**
- *Healthy growth:* Learning to own mistakes, developing humility
- *Trapped pattern:* Blame-shifting, defensiveness, needing to be right, inability to balance self and other care

**Parent (younger) — Hands-on Care:**
- *Healthy growth:* Developing sustainable care through rest and boundaries
- *Trapped pattern:* Burnout, resentment, over-functioning, martyr complex

**Parent (older) — Empowering & Releasing:**
- *Healthy growth:* Gradually releasing control and trusting others
- *Trapped pattern:* Micromanaging, inability to delegate, feeling threatened when others succeed

**Elder — Spiritual Wisdom:**
- *Healthy growth:* Growing into blessing, peace, and shared authority
- *Trapped pattern:* Holding power, seeking recognition, critical spirit, controlling through spiritual authority
